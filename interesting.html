What Is Country Music?

Country music is a genre rooted in the folk traditions of the American South and Appalachian mountains. Blending ballads, blues, and hymns, it emerged in the early 20th century as a distinctly American art form — one built on storytelling, heartache, faith, and everyday life.

From its early days as "hillbilly music" broadcast on radio barn dances, to the honky-tonk era of Hank Williams, through the outlaw movement of Waylon and Willie, and into today's streaming-driven mainstream dominance — country has always evolved while staying true to its soul.

Nashville, Tennessee remains the heart of the industry, home to the legendary Grand Ole Opry, Music Row, and a new generation of artists redefining what country can be.

Nashville, Tennessee — the undisputed capital of country music.

The Greatest Artists & Songs of All Time

  1. All-Time Greatest Artists
    • Johnny Cash — The Man in Black; iconic outlaw and country legend
    • Dolly Parton — Singer, songwriter, and humanitarian; one of the most decorated artists in history
    • Hank Williams Sr. — The father of modern country music
    • Willie Nelson — Outlaw pioneer whose career spans seven decades
    • Garth Brooks — The best-selling solo artist in U.S. history
    • Loretta Lynn — The Coal Miner's Daughter and trailblazer for women in country
    • Merle Haggard — Voice of the working class and Bakersfield Sound legend
  2. Modern Era Superstars
    • Morgan Wallen — Record-breaking streams and back-to-back #1 albums
    • Zach Bryan — Raw, authentic songwriting that has captured a new generation
    • Luke Combs — Known for record-breaking chart consistency
    • Kacey Musgraves — Grammy-winner pushing the genre's artistic boundaries
    • Chris Stapleton — Critically acclaimed vocalist and roots purist
  3. Greatest Songs of All Time
    • "I Will Always Love You" — Dolly Parton (1974)
    • "Ring of Fire" — Johnny Cash (1963)
    • "Friends in Low Places" — Garth Brooks (1990)
    • "The Dance" — Garth Brooks (1989)
    • "He Stopped Loving Her Today" — George Jones (1980)
    • "Jolene" — Dolly Parton (1973)
    • "Take Me Home, Country Roads" — John Denver (1971)
  4. Recent Chart-Toppers
    • "A Bar Song (Tipsy)" — Shaboozey (2024) — record-breaking crossover hit
    • "Fast Car" — Luke Combs (2023) — longest-running #1 of the decade
    • "I Remember Everything" — Zach Bryan ft. Kacey Musgraves (2023)
    • "Last Night" — Morgan Wallen (2023)
    • "Something in the Orange" — Zach Bryan (2022)
  5. Sub-Genres You Should Know
    • Classic Country — Traditional storytelling, steel guitar, fiddle
    • Outlaw Country — Anti-establishment, rough edges; think Waylon & Willie
    • Bluegrass — Acoustic, fast-picking roots music; Bill Monroe pioneered it
    • Bro-Country — Party anthems and mainstream crossover (2010s era)
    • Americana / Folk Country — Roots-forward, literary songwriting; Zach Bryan, Chris Stapleton
